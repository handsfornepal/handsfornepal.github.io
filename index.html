Nepal urgently needs COVID-19 vaccines

Nepal is currently facing the worst global wave of COVID-19 infections, with limited health services and access to vaccines.

Act Now

Why does Nepal need the vaccines?

  • {% include icons/site.html icon="population" %}

    Crisis prevention

    Only a small fraction of Nepal's 30 million people have been vaccinated and enough vaccines are not likely to be available until 2022. With a deadly second wave spreading across the country, a large number of people are at risk.

  • {% include icons/site.html icon="economy" %}

    Rising Cases

    Nepal is facing a steep rise in new COVID-19 cases, with a case-reproduction number that is higher than in neighboring India. Without immediate action, the current wave could spread throughout the country.

  • {% include icons/site.html icon="conditions" %}

    Access and Resources

    Conditions that could exacerbate the current crisis include: lack of enough hospital beds, healthcare providers and resources, urban areas with high population density, a long porous border with India, and Nepal falling out of the international radar.
