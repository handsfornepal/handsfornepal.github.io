Nepal urgently needs COVID-19 vaccines

Nepal is currently facing the worst global wave of COVID-19 infections, with limited health services and acute shortage of vaccines.

Act Now

Why does Nepal need COVID-19 vaccines?

  • {% include icons/site.html icon="population" %}

    Millions at Risk

    Only a small fraction of Nepal's 30 million people have been vaccinated and most of them have only received the first dose. Enough vaccines are unlikely to be available before 2022. A deadly second wave is spreading in Nepal, putting a large number of people at risk.

  • {% include icons/site.html icon="economy" %}

    Rising Cases

    Nepal is facing a steep rise in new COVID-19 cases, with a case-reproduction number that is among the highest in the world. The current wave in urban areas could spread throughout the country, once the strict lockdown restrictions start to loosen. This could lead to another wave of steeply rising cases.

  • {% include icons/site.html icon="conditions" %}

    Crisis Prevention

    Lack of enough hospital beds and healthcare resources, high population density, a long porous border with India, and waning international attention could exacerbate the situation. Immediate access to vaccines will prevent an impending crisis.

Our Mission

We are a global non-profit group working together for Nepal. Our group of volunteers includes scientists, practitioners, engineers, policy-makers, and industrial partners. Join us in urging governments, international partners, and vaccine providers to send relief to Nepal.

Everyone must have access to a COVID-19 vaccine. No one is safe until everyone is safe.
